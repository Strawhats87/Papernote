Key Drivers for Uro-Immunology Expansion:

Promunel® Profile

Promunel® is a standardized, patented propolis extract offering a unique dual-action approach for immune support, now being explored for uro-immunology.

What is Promunel®?

  • A polyphenol-rich propolis extract. Propolis is a resinous beehive product rich in flavonoids and phenolic acids.
  • Produced via the patented M.E.D.™ (Multi Dynamic Extraction) process, ensuring standardization and overcoming batch variability common in raw propolis17,18.
  • Contains a diverse profile of ~300 polyphenols11,48 including galangin, quercetin, chrysin, apigenin, pinocembrin, pinobanksin21.

Core Actions & Mechanisms:

  • Dual Action:
    1. Broad-spectrum Polyphenol Antimicrobial Effects.
    2. Immune Modulation (e.g., macrophage polarization).
  • This dual action potentially reduces infection recurrence and inflammation-driven symptoms.

Key Differentiators & USPs:

  • Macrophage Polarization: FYTEXIA's patent (FR3154315) supports Promunel®'s ability to modulate innate immunity by shifting macrophages from pro-inflammatory M1 to anti-inflammatory M2 state5,14,15. This is highly relevant for bladder mucosa health.
  • Standardization: M.E.D.™ process ensures consistent polyphenol profile and efficacy19.
  • Broad-Spectrum Effects: Unlike single-target cranberry or D-mannose.
  • Goes Beyond Cranberry: Offers a new, science-driven alternative in a market seeking innovation.

Proven Efficacy (Respiratory Health):

  • Demonstrated a 29% reduction in respiratory infections (colds vs placebo) in a recent RCT4,31.
  • Indicates a broad immune-support role, extendable to uro-immunology.
  • Analogous to OM-85 (Broncho-Vaxom) for respiratory immunity, hinting at propolis as a natural "immunostimulant" across mucosal sites.

Scientific Rationale for UTI Prevention

Promunel®'s multi-faceted actions target both the pathogen ("the bug") and the host's defenses, setting it apart from conventional UTI supplements.

1. Immune Modulation:

  • Promotes anti-inflammatory macrophage phenotype (M1 to M2 shift)5,14,15.
  • Regulates cytokines (e.g., lowers TNF-α via miR-19a-3p, miR-203a-3p upregulation)6,13.
  • May reduce chronic inflammatory milieu post-rUTIs, alleviate symptoms, and improve bacterial clearance.
  • Addresses underlying immune deficits and microbiota disturbances often seen in rUTI-prone women16.

2. Anti-Adhesion & Anti-Biofilm:

  • Propolis polyphenols (flavonoids like galangin, quercetin etc.21) may inhibit biofilm formation and motility of uropathogens20.
  • Crucial as UPEC often forms biofilms, evading antibiotics.
  • Propolis can potentiate cranberry's anti-adhesion effect against UPEC, amplifying effects on motility and biofilm genes7,8.
  • Suggests propolis flavonoids may inhibit bacterial quorum-sensing or virulence pathways.

3. Direct Antimicrobial Action:

  • Exhibits bacteriostatic/bactericidal effects against various microbes11.
  • M.E.D.™ propolis is effective against drug-resistant strains19, valuable for rUTI patients with multi-drug resistant E. coli.
  • Gentler than antibiotics, potentially suppressing pathogen load without disrupting beneficial flora (needs urinary context study).

4. Antioxidant & Tissue Protective:

  • rUTIs cause oxidative stress in bladder tissue.
  • Propolis polyphenols are potent antioxidants.
  • Reported upregulation of NRF2-related antioxidant responses11,22.
  • May protect urothelial cells, maintain mucosal barrier integrity, and reduce hypersensitivity.

Overall Mechanistic Rationale:

Promunel® addresses both sides of the UTI equation:
- The Bug: Lowers virulence (anti-adhesion, anti-biofilm, direct antimicrobial).
- The Host: Boosts defensive capacity (immune modulation, antioxidant, tissue protection).
This polyphenol-driven antimicrobial action + immune modulation is the cornerstone of its UTI application5.

Regulatory Outlook & Clinical Trial Concept

While direct UTI prevention claims are challenging in the EU, Promunel® can leverage general health and immune support claims, bolstered by a strong clinical trial.

EU Regulatory Landscape (EFSA Health Claims):

  • No Article 13.1 generic claim for "urinary tract health" authorized.
  • Past Article 13.5 applications for UTI-related claims (e.g., cranberry PACs "defence against pathogens") rejected due to insufficient cause-effect evidence10.
    • E.g., Pacran® review (2025 EFSA) acknowledged physiological effect (adhesion inhibition) but no definitive UTI risk reduction10,23.
  • Direct prevention/recurrence claims are considered disease-risk reduction, requiring pharmaceutical-level evidence.
  • Nuance: National traditions/on-hold botanical claims. France approved "36mg PAC cranberry can help reduce adhesion of E. coli"24 (not EU-wide). Some traditional herbal medicinal products (e.g., Ellura for cystitis recurrence25) exist.

Promunel® Regulatory Strategy:

  • Position as a food supplement.
  • Utilize general health claims (e.g., "contributes to normal function of the immune system" if combined with Vitamin C/D/Zinc).
  • Use structure-function phrasing (e.g., "supports urinary tract health," "helps maintain bladder comfort") in marketing (with scientific context, no explicit disease prevention).
  • Leverage "on-hold" botanical claims for propolis (e.g., "natural defenses") where applicable per country.

Clinical Trial Concept for rUTI Prevention:

  • Objective: Substantiate efficacy in preventing UTIs and support marketing claims.
  • Target Population: Adult women (18-70 yrs) with recurrent UTIs (≥3 UTIs in past 12 months or ≥2 in last 6 months49).
  • Study Design: Randomized, double-blind, controlled trial (parallel groups), multicenter in Europe.
    • Arm A: Promunel® supplement (48 mg/day propolis polyphenols).
    • Arm B: Comparator - leading cranberry extract (e.g., 36 mg PAC/day, like Ellura).
    • (Optional Arm C: Placebo - ethically challenging but strengthens claims).
  • Dosage & Administration: E.g., 2 capsules of 100mg Promunel® extract daily. Comparator: 1 cap daily.
  • Study Duration: 6 months supplementation, follow-up to 9-12 months.
  • Primary Endpoints:
    1. UTI Recurrence Rate (symptomatic, culture-confirmed UTIs per person-year).
    2. Time to First UTI (Kaplan-Meier).
    3. Symptom Severity/Frequency (validated questionnaire).
  • Secondary Endpoints: Inflammation/Immune Markers (CRP, urinary IL-6, IL-8), Antibiotic Use, Microbiological changes (vaginal/urinary flora), Quality of Life (QoL).
  • Estimated Sample Size: ~100-150 participants per arm (total ~250-300).
  • Estimated Cost: €250k-€500k (pilot ~€100k).
  • Impact: Non-inferiority or superiority to cranberry would be landmark. Data for publications, marketing, potential EFSA/national claims. "Propolis vs Cranberry head-to-head."
